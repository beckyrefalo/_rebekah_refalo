Midnight Parliament

Valletta to Host First-Ever "Midnight Parliament" in Bid to Boost Youth Engagement VALLETTA – In an unprecedented move, the Maltese government has announced the launch of “Midnight Parliament,” a late-night legislative session aimed at involving university students and young professionals in live political debate. Prime Minister Leanne Mifsud said the initiative will begin as a trial in September, with debates livestreamed on national TV. Critics argue it’s a populist stunt, while supporters hail it as “a bold experiment in democratic accessibility.” “We need to meet citizens where they are — even if that’s at midnight,” Mifsud said.

Floating Solar Panels in Gżira

Gżira Residents Report ‘Humming Sky’ as Solar Energy Project Enters Phase Two GŻIRA – A low, persistent hum reported by dozens of residents this week has been traced to Malta’s largest floating solar platform, currently undergoing expansion in Marsamxett Harbour. The €90 million project, operated by SunWave Energy, aims to supply 15% of the island’s energy needs by 2027. But locals say the sound is affecting sleep and causing anxiety. An inquiry has been launched. “It sounds like the sea is whispering in Morse code,” said one resident.

Mdina

Heritage Malta Discovers Secret Chamber Beneath Mdina Cathedral MDINA – Archaeologists with Heritage Malta have uncovered a previously undocumented chamber beneath St. Paul’s Cathedral, believed to date back to the 13th century. The chamber, discovered during restoration works, contains early Latin inscriptions and what appears to be a mosaic map of a forgotten port. A team of conservators and historians is currently assessing the site. Rumors of a hidden chamber have circulated for years, though many dismissed them as urban legend. “This changes everything,” said lead archaeologist Dr. Keith Aquilina.

Gozo Ferry

Maltese Island to Test First AI-Operated Ferry Between Gozo and Valletta VALLETTA – Transport Malta has unveiled an ambitious pilot project: the island’s first AI-operated ferry, set to run autonomously between Gozo and Valletta by early 2026. Nicknamed Ċikka, the solar-powered vessel will undergo three months of closed trials starting in October. Authorities claim the ferry can reduce carbon emissions by 40% and cut commute times. While transport advocates have welcomed the innovation, seafarers’ unions are calling for guarantees about job security. “We welcome progress, but not at the cost of tradition and livelihood,” said union rep Joseph Galea.

Fort Ricasoli

Storm Uncovers 17th-Century Tunnel Linking Fort Ricasoli to Birgu KALKARA – Last week’s unseasonal storm has led to the accidental discovery of a collapsed 17th-century tunnel system, believed to have connected Fort Ricasoli to Birgu during the Knights’ period. Civil protection teams found the tunnel after part of the shoreline near Rinella Bay gave way, revealing sandstone arches and hand-carved passageways. Initial surveys suggest it may have served as an emergency supply route during the Great Siege of 1565.

Lottery

National Lottery Numbers Accidentally Predicted in Local Play, Causes Public Frenzy MOSTA – Chaos erupted at a local theatre on Saturday after it emerged that a fictional lottery number drawn during the second act of the satirical play Il-Milied tal-Maħfra matched all six numbers of that evening’s actual national lottery draw. The theatre group claims it was pure coincidence, but social media users have dubbed the playwright “the Nostradamus of Mosta.” The incident has drawn crowds to the theatre and a small police investigation into betting irregularities — no wrongdoing has been found.

Dingli Cliffs

Rare Albino Blue Rock Thrush Spotted in Dingli Cliffs, Birdwatchers Rejoice DINGLI – A rare albino Merill (Blue Rock Thrush), Malta’s national bird, was sighted on Dingli Cliffs this morning by a group of amateur birdwatchers. Ornithologists confirm it is the first albino specimen recorded on the island. BirdLife Malta has cordoned off the area and urged photographers to keep their distance. “This is like seeing a unicorn,” said avian biologist Dr. Lara Schembri.
